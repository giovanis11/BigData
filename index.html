01 The 4Vs of Big Data

Volume is amount. Velocity is speed. Variety is diversity of formats. Veracity is trust, quality, accuracy, integrity, and credibility.

The instructor materials also use the classic “3Vs” shorthand: Volume, Velocity, and Variety. The reading adds Veracity as the fourth dimension.

02 Big Data vs Small Data

Big Data is large, complex, constantly changing, sourced widely, and able to absorb unstructured data. It usually needs experts, cleaning, distributed storage, and new workflows.

Small Data is closer to end users, often structured, easier to translate into information and business intelligence, and commonly analyzed in spreadsheets.

03 Management, Analysis & Storage

Data management collects, stores, organizes, and maintains accurate, available, accessible data. Data analysis transforms data into useful information. Data mining searches for hidden patterns and trends.

Databases connect related files. Forms input data; queries retrieve it. Warehouses consolidate current and historical data for analysis. Lakes hold raw structured, semi-structured, and unstructured data without a predefined schema.

04 Analytics in Practice

Know the use cases: predictive analytics, fraud detection, personalized marketing, recommendation systems, sentiment analysis, healthcare, energy management, smart cities, and IoT analytics.

The reading adds applications in e-commerce, education, banking, telecommunications, and government services.

05 Visual Analytics

Visualization turns raw data into charts, graphs, images, or video so viewers can understand patterns and trends. Visual analytics helps people explore, detect, compare, and explain.

Anscombe’s Quartet shows why plots matter: four datasets share nearly identical summary statistics but reveal dramatically different patterns when visualized.

06 How Visuals Communicate

Semiotics studies meaning in signs, symbols, colors, and shapes. Preattentive attributes such as color, size, shape, position, orientation, and length are noticed almost instantly.

Gestalt principles describe natural grouping: proximity, similarity, closure, and continuity. Apply them deliberately and avoid false grouping.

07 Data Literacy Cases

Behavioral reflection is data created by actions, such as choosing a Netflix movie. Direct input is data a user deliberately provides, such as rating that movie.

Social media improves connection and access but requires careful vetting. The Yale alumni income example shows how self-reporting, non-response, and selection bias can inflate a result.

08 Data Scale

The slide sequence is Byte, Kilobyte, Megabyte, Gigabyte, Terabyte, Petabyte, Exabyte, Zettabyte. Each step shown is 1,024 of the preceding unit.

Examples from the deck: a byte is one character; a megabyte can hold a 20-slide PowerPoint; a terabyte can hold roughly 300 hours of good-quality video.

09 GDPR Essentials

GDPR is an enforceable EU regulation governing personal data. It can apply to organizations anywhere when they target or collect data relating to people in the EU.

Personal data identifies a person directly or indirectly. A data controller decides why and how processing occurs. A processor handles data for a controller. A data subject is the person the data concerns.

10 Principles & Legal Bases

The seven principles are lawfulness, fairness and transparency; purpose limitation; data minimization; accuracy; storage limitation; integrity and confidentiality; and accountability.

Processing needs a legal basis: consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public interest or official function, or legitimate interests.

11 Rights, Consent & Breaches

Know the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, and rights relating to automated decision-making and profiling.

Consent must be freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous, clear, documented, and withdrawable. A breach includes accidental or unlawful loss, deletion, alteration, disclosure, or access.

12 ACG Policy Activity

ACG is the data controller. Faculty, staff, and students share responsibility for following its policy. A suspected breach should be reported immediately to the Data Protection Officer at dpo@acg.edu.

A student who needs to process personal data for study must obtain written tutor or supervisor agreement, inform the DPO, and refer to the IRB when human participants are involved.

13 AI & Data Literacy

AI learns patterns from training data and adjusts parameters to produce later outputs. Weak AI performs limited tasks; Strong AI is theoretical human-level general intelligence; Super AI is speculative intelligence beyond human capability.

Data literacy helps assess training data, interpret outputs, make informed decisions, and reason about bias, privacy, and ethics.

14 Cybersecurity & Smart Cities

Cybersecurity protects systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, damage, and theft. Big Data and AI depend on data availability, confidentiality, and integrity.

The six smart-city themes are living, economy, people, governance, mobility, and environment. Connected systems improve services while raising privacy concerns through continual collection.

15 Visual Storytelling

Group related infographic items together. Use context and clear comparisons. The Law of Prägnanz says people interpret complex visual information in the simplest way possible.

Bar charts support precise comparison through a common baseline. Pie charts show proportions but become harder to compare as slices multiply. Keep related comparisons close and use consistent scales, colors, and labels.

16 Expanded Gestalt Principles

Week 2 extends the visual toolkit: proximity, similarity, enclosure, connection, continuity, closure, figure-ground, and common fate. These describe how viewers group elements before consciously analyzing a graphic.

Preattentive attributes such as color, shape, and intensity can strengthen the signal or accidentally add noise.
